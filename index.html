My Favorite Characters by Franchise


Ben 10

Ben Tennyson - the main character, a boy who wields an alien watch-like device called the Omnitrix that enables him to transform into different alien beings, each with their unique set of powers and abilities

Gwen Tennyson - Ben's cousin who inherited Anodite heritage; manipulates mana to cast spells, form energy constructs and forcefields, heal, teleport, and can assume a powerful Anodite form

Heatblast - a Pyronite with fire and heat manipulation. From the planet Pyros.

Diamondhead - a Petrosapien with invulnerability and crystal manipulation. From the planet Petropia.

Grey Matter - a Galvan with small size and super intelligence. From the planet Galvan Prime.

XLR8 - a Kineceleran with super speed. From the planet Kinet.

Four Arms - a Tetramand with four arms and super strength. From the planet Khoros.

Upgrade - a Galvanic Mechamorph with shape-shifting, technology merging, enhancement, and manipulation. From planet Galvan Prime's moon, Galvan B.

Ripjaws - a Piscciss Volann with underwater prowess, sharp teeth, and jaws that can tear through almost anything. From the planet Piscciss.

Ghostfreak - an Ectonurite with flight, invisibility, intangibility, fear manipulation, and possession. Originally from the planet Anur Phaetos, the Ectonurites now live on planet Anur Transyl.

Wildvine - a Florauna with elasticity and plant control. Originally from the planet Flors Verdance, the Floraunas now live on planet Xenon.

Way Big - a To'kustar with cosmic size, super strength, reflexes, and cosmic energy manipulation. To'kustars are created via cosmic storms.

Kevin 11 (from Ben 10: Alien Force, Ben 10: Ultimate Alien, and Ben 10: Omniverse) - can absorb the properties of any material he touches, as well as absorb the biological powers of any superpowered being he touches

Humungosaur - a huge Vaxasaurian with super strength and the ability to grow larger. From the planet Terradino.

Jetray - an Aerophibian with flight, super speed, underwater breathing, and neuroshock beams. From the planet Aeropela.

Big Chill - a Necrofriggian with flight, invisibility, intangibility, and cryokinesis via ice breath. Originally from the planet Kylmyys, the Necrofriggians now live on planet Mikd'lty.

Spidermonkey - an Arachnichimp with four arms, agility, surface-sticking, and web-spinning from its tail. From the planet Aranhascimmia.

Brainstorm - a Cerebrocrustacean with super intelligence and psionic lightning projection. From the planet Encephalonus IV.

Alien X - a Celestialsapien with nigh-omnipotence. Celestialsapiens are from the Forge of Creation.

Lodestar - a Biotsavartian with magnetokinesis and metal manipulation. From the planet Maxwell.

Shocksquatch - a Gimlinopithecus with super strength, electrokinesis, electrical force fields, and telekinesis via electrical force fields. From the planet Pattersonea.

Feedback - a Conductoid with energy absorption, redirection, and manipulation. From the Teslavorr nebula.

Gravattack - a Galilean with gravity manipulation, force fields, and telekinesis via force fields. From planet Keplorr's orbit.

Bloxx - a Segmentasapien with shape-shifting and body-altering and fragmenting. From the planet Polyominus.

Alan Albright – a human infused with Pyronite DNA, allowing him to generate and control fire.

Azmuth - a Galvan from Galvan Prime and the creator of the Omnitrix. Renowned as one of the universe's most brilliant minds, he designed the Omnitrix to promote peace by allowing species to experience each other's lives

Bahrvad - a Tetramand gladiator from the planet Khoros, known for heavy strength and four-armed combat.

Baz-L - a Galvanic Mechamorph, like Upgrade from Galvan Prime's moon, Galvan B, with shape-shifting and techno-morphing abilities.

Ben 10,000 - an alternate future version of Ben Tennyson who, in Ben 10: Omniverse, developed the Biomnitrix, a dual-Omnitrix system that allows him to fuse the DNA of two aliens into a single, hybrid form

Ben 23 - a version of Ben from Dimension 23, characterized by his use of the "Hero Watch" and a more self-serving approach to heroism

Blukic - a Galvan technician who, along with Driba, assists the Plumbers with technology and gadgets. He is slightly taller and thinner than the average Galvan

Driba - a Galvan technician working alongside Blukic. He is shorter and stockier, with a prominent chin and flat head, differing from most Galvans

Fridge — a Necrofriggian who was enlisted by Albedo to impersonate Big Chill during a staged show; looks like Big Chill but with a larger belly and a longer cloak.

Fourmungosaur - a fusion of Four Arms and Humungousaur's DNA, accessible only through the Biomnitrix. He has the body of Humungousaur with Four Arms' features, including four muscular arms, red skin,, spikes, and two pairs of eyes. Only appears in the Ben 10: Omniverse episode "And Then There Were None"

Gar Red Wind - a bulky Tetramand warlord and Looma Red Wind’s father, renowned for brute strength and ruling his territory.

Gwen 10 – An alternate timeline version of Gwen Tennyson who ends up with the Omnitrix instead of Ben. She uses it to transform into alien forms and fight powerful threats, showing skill, courage, and quick thinking in battle.

Haplor - an Arachnachimp who closely resembles Spidermonkey; family-oriented and seen with a wife (or mate, whatever you want to call her) and daughter in Ben 10: Ultimate Alien.

Helen Wheels - a human imbued with Kineceleran DNA, granting her super-speed abilities

Hugh — a Vaxasaurian chosen by Albedo to play Humungousaur on Ben 10: Live; resembles Humungousaur but lacks the Omnitrix/Ultimatrix mark.

Hugo — Professor Paradox’s assistant from the 1950s who was accidentally transformed into a trans-dimensional creature during a time-travel experiment.

Magister Patelliday — a Piscciss Volann Plumber, second-in-command of the Plumbers of Earth.

Magister Pyke — a Piscciss Volann Magister who appears as a Plumber and leader of his people in the series.

Manny Armstrong - a human with Tetramand DNA, giving him enhanced strength and durability

Mechamorph Armor - a suit of Galvanic Mechamorph-based armor created by Azmuth, allowing the wearer to merge with and control technology

Mechamorph Guard - a large Galvanic Mechamorph who serves as Azmuth’s guardian, capable of size and shape alteration

Mizaru - a minor antagonist in Ben 10: Ultimate Alien, an Arachnichimp crime boss who clashes with Ben in “Simian Says.”

Mother Celestialsapien — the unnamed Celestialsapien mother of the Star Child, living in the Forge of Creation.

Pax — a Florauna activist from Flors Verdance who appears in Ben 10: Omniverse

Princess Looma Red Wind - a Tetramand princess from planet Khoros and daughter of Gar Red Wind; a towering, four-armed warrior famed for exceptional strength and combat skill, and briefly betrothed to Ben after a duel.

Professor Paradox - a time-traveling scientist with control over space-time. Once trapped in the Void for millennia, he gained vast knowledge and temporal powers, often guiding Ben during major cosmic threats

Ship - a Galvanic Mechamorph, akin to Upgrade. He assists Julie Yamamoto and has the ability to merge with and enhance technology

Simian - an Arachnachimp con artist and villainous smuggler, clever and slippery.

Starbeard - a muscular Celestialsapien mystic with a long beard and balanced dual consciousness, wielding cosmic staff powers

Star Child — an infant Celestialsapien with a star-filled, night-sky body and emerging cosmic powers; appeared in "The Forge of Creation," where Ultimate Aggregor tried to absorb its power since it hadn't developed multiple personalities yet.

Suemungousaur — a female Vaxasaurian resembling Humungousaur.

T8-M — a Kineceleran (ML-E’s mother) who lives in Undertown

Tetrax Shard - a Petrosapien, like Diamondhead. He is a bounty hunter known for his crystalline body and combat skills

The Galactic Gladiator - a heavily built Celestialsapien arena warrior, clad in armor and famed for gladiatorial prowess

Tini - a female Tetramand from the planet Khoros, like Four Arms

Verdona – a full-fledged Anodite and Gwen’s grandmother; an ancient mana being who wields potent mana manipulation for beams, constructs, and advanced magic, far more experienced than Gwen

Yetta - a polite but thieving Gimlinopithecus crook from Ben 10: Omniverse, often apologetic despite her crimes.

Albedo – a Galvan scientist-turned-clone of Ben Tennyson who stole Omnitrix tech; mirrors Ben’s powers with cold intellect and selfish schemes.

Heatbat — a bat mutated by Dr. Animo with Pyronite (Heatblast) DNA; gained heat/fire-based attacks and fought Ben and the team.

Zs'Skayr/Ghostfreak - an Ectonurite, a species known for their ghostly appearance and abilities. Zs'Skayr is the evil counterpart of Ghostfreak.

Malware - a Galvanic Mechamorph with malevolent intentions. He can merge with and corrupt technology, posing a significant threat

Khyber - a ruthless and skilled hunter who uses the Nemetrix, a device that turns him into predator versions of Ben’s aliens. He hunts powerful beings for sport and sees Ben as his ultimate prey

Dr. Psychobos – A psychotic, cybernetically enhanced Cerebrocrustacean scientist and major villain. Obsessed with proving his intellect superior to Azmuth and the Galvans, he invented the Nemetrix and uses electricity, tech genius, and cunning schemes against Ben and the Plumbers.

Bad Ben - an evil Ben Tennyson from an alternate timeline in Ben 10: Omniverse



The Elder Scrolls

Last Dragonborn/Dovahkiin (end of The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim) – A legendary hero born with the soul of a dragon and destined to slay Alduin. By the end of Skyrim, the Dragonborn becomes a godlike being capable of defeating world-ending threats, absorbing dragon souls, and mastering the Thu'um and various forms of magic to manipulate reality. They are not bound by their universe's structure and can perform feats like instant kills, resurrecting allies, or manipulating reality itself. The Dovahkiin has immeasurable speed, countless hax, and numerous artifacts like Mehrunes Razor, Keening, the Ghostblade, and the Staff of Magnus, all contributing to their combat versatility.

Paarthurnax – An ancient dragon and former lieutenant of Alduin who turned against him. He teaches the Way of the Voice to mortals and seeks redemption through peace and wisdom.

The Greybeards (Arngeir, Borri, Einarth, and Wulfgar) – Monks who live in High Hrothgar and devote their lives to mastering and preserving the Way of the Voice. They guide the Dragonborn in their journey to understand the Thu’um.

Serana Volkihar – A powerful vampire and daughter of Lord Harkon. Caught between her family’s dark ambitions and her own conscience, she allies with the Dragonborn to stop her father. Skilled in vampiric magic and combat, she is loyal, witty, and surprisingly compassionate despite her undead nature.

Alduin – The World-Eater, a god-like dragon prophesied to destroy the world. He seeks to dominate all creation but is ultimately defeated by the Dovahkiin in Skyrim.

Miraak (the first Dragonborn) – The first mortal to be gifted the Thu’um by the dragons. He betrayed them and sought power through Hermaeus Mora. He is encountered and defeated in the Dragonborn DLC.

Akatosh – The Dragon God of Time and chief deity of the Divines. Creator of the Dragonborn and father of time, he represents order and endurance in the cosmos.

The Altmer, or High Elves – A proud and magically gifted race from Summerset Isle. Known for their arrogance and skill in arcane arts, they often consider themselves superior to other races.

The Nerevarine - the protagonist of The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind. A prophesied reincarnation of the ancient hero Nerevar, destined to unite the tribes of Morrowind, defeat the godlike Dagoth Ur, and restore balance by mastering powerful magic, ancient secrets, and legendary combat skills.

Dagoth Ur - the antagonist of The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind. A godlike, immortal sorcerer corrupted by the Heart of Lorkhan, seeking to reshape Morrowind under his rule and spread his divine plague, ultimately defeated by the Nerevarine.

Anu – The primordial embodiment of stasis and order. Represents the essence of light and time, and is one of the original cosmic forces in Elder Scrolls mythology.

Anui-El – A reflection and extension of Anu. Serves as the soul of all things and the source of the Aedra, including Akatosh.

Hermaeus Mora – The Daedric Prince of knowledge and memory. A tentacled being who hoards forbidden knowledge and seeks to control all information in existence.

Jyggalag – The Daedric Prince of order. A towering figure of crystal and steel, embodying perfect logic and absolute structure, seeking to impose unchanging order on the whole of existence.

Lord Harkon – The vampire lord antagonist of the Dawnguard DLC.

Lorkhan – The trickster god who persuaded the Aedra to create the mortal world. His actions led to his betrayal and the removal of his heart, which still influences events across Tamriel.

Magnus – The god of magic who designed Mundus, the mortal plane, but fled before its completion. His departure created the sun, and his essence powers the use of magic.

Mehrunes Dagon – The Daedric Prince of destruction, change, and ambition. A fiery, horned demon who revels in chaos, war, and the overthrow of civilizations.

Meridia – The Daedric Prince of life, energy, and the undead. A radiant, incorporeal being who opposes necromancy and seeks to purify the world of corruption and death.

Namira – The Daedric Prince of darkness, decay, and all repulsive things. Revered by outcasts and those who embrace filth, rot, and the perverse.

Padomay – The primordial embodiment of chaos and change. Opposite to Anu, he represents instability and the potential for creation through conflict.

Satakal – A Yokudan deity who embodies the cyclical nature of destruction and rebirth. Viewed as the world and the devourer of the world.

Sotha Sil – One of the Tribunal gods. A brilliant inventor and mage, he created the Clockwork City and sought to understand and manipulate reality through logic and machinery.

Talos, or Tiber Septim – A mortal who ascended to godhood and became one of the Nine Divines. United Tamriel under the Empire and embodies heroism, conquest, and divine rule.

Vivec – A god-king of the Tribunal, known for his poetic and contradictory nature. He merged aspects of mortal and divine and ruled Morrowind with godly power and mysticism.



Dragon Ball

Goku - a pure-hearted Saiyan raised on Earth. Known for his love of fighting strong opponents, he’s mastered many forms, including Ultra Instinct

Vegeta - the proud Saiyan prince. Once a villain, now a hero fighting to surpass Goku. Known for his fiery temper and intense training

Gohan (adult) - The eldest son of Goku and Chi-Chi. Intelligent and powerful, especially in his Ultimate form, though he prefers peace over fighting.

Piccolo - a wise and powerful Namekian warrior. Former enemy turned mentor and protector, especially close to Gohan and Pan

Krillin – A brave and loyal human martial artist. Goku’s best friend, known for his clever techniques like the Destructo Disc, and his determination despite being outmatched by many foes.

Yamcha - a former desert bandit turned Z Fighter. Skilled in martial arts and ki techniques like the Wolf Fang Fist, but often outclassed by stronger foes

Bulma Briefs - a brilliant inventor and co-founder of Capsule Corp. Daughter of Dr. Briefs, the company’s founder. One of Goku’s oldest friends, Vegeta’s wife, and mother of Trunks. Key in creating useful tech

Kid Chi-Chi – the daughter of the Ox-King. Trained in martial arts, she’s shy and sweet but fiercely protective, and promises her childhood friend Goku that he’ll marry her.

Videl (Dragon Ball Z) - A strong-willed martial artist and daughter of Mr. Satan. She marries Gohan and becomes Pan’s mother.

Android 18 – A former human turned android by Dr. Gero. Calm, clever, and strong, she fights when needed and cares deeply for her family, especially her husband Krillin.

Majin Buu (Good Buu/Fat Buu) – The kind-hearted, chubby version of Majin Buu. Loves sweets and becomes a friend and ally to the Z Fighters.

Pan - the granddaughter of Goku and daughter of Gohan and Videl. A young Saiyan-Human hybrid with great potential, energetic and determined.

Bulla (Bra) – The cheerful daughter of Vegeta and Bulma. Playful and curious, she shows early signs of Saiyan potential and enjoys spending time with her family.

Frieza - a ruthless galactic emperor known for destroying Planet Vegeta, the Saiyans' home planet. Has multiple powerful forms and a long-standing rivalry with Goku and Vegeta.

Nappa - a brutal Saiyan warrior and Vegeta’s former partner. Powerful during his debut but ultimately defeated by Goku.

Vegito – a fusion of Goku and Vegeta using the Potara earrings. Known for overwhelming strength and flashy techniques like the Final Kamehameha.

Broly (Dragon Ball Z) - a Legendary Super Saiyan with overwhelming power and a deep hatred for Goku. Known for his berserker rage and near-limitless strength.

Super Janemba - a powerful demon born from corrupted energy in the afterlife. Capable of warping reality, he is defeated by Gogeta, the fusion of Goku and Vegeta achieved by performing the Fusion Dance.

Bacterian - a disgusting martial artist who uses his foul stench as a weapon. Faced off against Krillin in the original World Martial Arts Tournament.

Bardock - Goku's father and a low-class Saiyan with a strong will. Gained visions of Frieza’s betrayal and tried to stop him before Planet Vegeta's destruction.

Caulifla – a bold and rebellious Saiyan from Universe 6. A natural prodigy with huge potential, she learns Super Saiyan forms quickly and loves pushing her strength further, driven by confidence, curiosity, and a fierce desire to grow stronger.

Gine - Goku’s gentle and caring mother. Unlike most Saiyans, she values family and emotion over battle.

Broly (Dragon Ball Super) - a reimagined version of Broly who has a tragic backstory. His raw power surpasses most fighters, and he becomes an ally after battling Goku and Vegeta.

Cheelai - a kind-hearted former Frieza soldier who befriends Broly. She helps rescue him from Frieza's control and supports Broly's new life.

Legendary Super Saiyan 2 Kefla - the fused warrior of Caulifla and Kale (Saiyan warriors from Universe 6) taken to the next level. Her power is immense, combining Kale’s rage and Caulifla’s aggression.

Hit – A legendary assassin from Universe 6. Uses time-skipping techniques to strike with precision and efficiency, calm and calculating in every battle.

Ranfan – A female martial artist who competed in the World Martial Arts Tournament. Uses distraction tactics in battle but lacks serious combat power. Bold and confident.

Xeno Goku (base and Super Saiyan 4 Limit Breaker forms) - A time-patrol version of Goku from the Dragon Ball Heroes timeline. Uses Super Saiyan 4 Limit Breaker form, granting immense power in defending the timelines.

Xeno Vegeta (Super Saiyan 4 Limit Breaker) - A time-patrol version of Vegeta from the Dragon Ball Heroes timeline. Fights alongside Xeno Goku and reaches Limit Breaker form, rivaling gods in strength.

Xeno Broly - a version of Broly from the Dragon Ball Heroes timeline. More controlled and even more powerful, often used in multiversal threats.

Zalama - the divine being who created the Super Dragon Balls. Extremely mysterious and possibly one of the most powerful entities in the Dragon Ball multiverse.



Transformers

Optimus Prime - The noble Autobot leader. Calm, compassionate, and unwavering, he leads through example and courage.

Optimus Prime (with the Matrix of Leadership) - Empowered by the Matrix. Radiates wisdom and authority, inspiring allies with legendary strength.

Bumblebee – A quick and clever Autobot scout. Brave and friendly, always ready to help.

Arcee – A skilled female Autobot warrior. Agile and determined with strong loyalty.

Grimlock – The powerful Dinobot leader with a fierce T-Rex mode. Strong-willed and battle-hungry.

Blurr - A lightning-fast Autobot messenger. Sharp-tongued and quick-witted with unmatched speed.

Cosmos - an Autobot whose alternate mode is a UFO

Glyph - A small, intelligent Autobot known for her linguistic and archaeological expertise. Prefers research over combat but is capable when needed.

Highbrow - A scholarly Autobot who values logic and knowledge. Transforms into a twin-rotor helicopter and often analyzes problems from an intellectual angle.

Hot Rod – A brave Autobot with a fiery spirit. Quick, skilled, and destined for leadership.

Metroplex - A colossal Autobot city-bot. Serves as a mobile base and powerhouse in battle, often silent but fiercely protective of his allies.

Perceptor - super-intelligent Autobot with a microscope alternate mode

Prowl – A strategic Autobot tactician known for his calm, logical mind. Skilled in planning and analysis, he values order and efficiency, often acting as Optimus Prime’s second-in-command.

Rewind - A small Autobot archivist and data collector. Often partners with Eject and is known for his vast memory banks and analytical mind.

Seaspray - An Autobot who transforms into a hovercraft. Known for his love of the sea and his distinct gurgling voice due to his aquatic form.

Sky Lynx - A proud and boastful Autobot who transforms into a space shuttle and lynx-like beast. Highly capable and values his own eloquence and achievements.

Springer - an Autobot who is also a Wrecker with two alternate modes: a land vehicle and a helicopter vehicle with wheels.

Megatron - The ruthless Decepticon leader. Powerful, intelligent, and driven by domination.

Megatron (Dark Energon-powered) - Corrupted by Dark Energon. Far more savage and destructive, fueled by raw, unstable power.

Starscream - A scheming Decepticon Seeker. Ambitious, arrogant, and treacherous, always seeking command.

Starscream (Underbase) - Supercharged by the Underbase. Vastly amplified power and ambition, nearly uncontrollable.

Soundwave – A loyal Decepticon spy and communications expert. Calm, calculating, with deadly precision.

Skywarp - A Decepticon Seeker who transforms into a jet. Known for his teleportation ability and cowardly, prank-loving nature. Loyal to Megatron but often unreliable.

Sixshot - A powerful Decepticon with six different transformations. Known as a one-robot army, he's cold, efficient, and nearly unstoppable in combat.

Optimus Primal - leader of the heroic Maximals. Transforms into a gorilla.

Optimus Minor – A spirited Maximal scout and warrior. Transforms into a spider monkey.

B'Boom - a Maximal whose beast mode is a mandrill

Claw Jaw – a stealthy and agile Maximal. Transforms into a giant squid.

Escargon – a clever and resilient Maximal. Transforms into a giant snail.

Megatron (Beast Wars) – leader of the villainous Predacons. Transforms into a fearsome Tyrannosaurus rex.

Tarantulas – A cunning Predacon scientist and schemer. Transforms into a venomous tarantula. Known for his intelligence, cruelty, and obsession with experiments.

Trypticon - A massive Decepticon city-bot. Transforms into a battle station or dinosaur-like form, often used for destruction and conquest.

Gas Skunk – A devious Predacon warrior. Transforms into a skunk. Known for his foul chemical attacks, stealthy sabotage, and unpredictable, nasty temperament.

Primus - The creator god of the Transformers, embodying order and light. Opposes Unicron and created Cybertron along with the original Transformers.

Unicron - A malevolent god of chaos and destruction. Transforms into a planet-sized being and seeks to consume worlds, including Cybertron.

Prima - The first Transformer created by Primus. Wields the Star Saber and represents leadership, valor, and righteousness among the original Thirteen.

Vector Prime - One of the original Thirteen Transformers. Guardian of time and space, he ensures the multiverse remains balanced and stable.



Others:

Asura from Asura’s Wrath – A demigod fueled by rage and vengeance. Possesses godlike strength and resilience, able to take on gods themselves while driven by the desire to protect his daughter and seek justice.

Earthworm Jim – A normal earthworm transformed into a super-powered hero by a robotic suit. Witty, unpredictable, and fearless, he battles bizarre villains across strange worlds.

Fulgore from Killer Instinct – A cybernetic soldier built for combat. Equipped with advanced weaponry and AI-enhanced reflexes, he’s a relentless, tactical killing machine.

Glacius from Killer Instinct – A sentient alien made of living ice. Uses freezing attacks and shape-shifting abilities to dominate foes with precision and control.

King K. Rool from Donkey Kong Country – The ruthless Kremling king and arch-nemesis of the Kongs. Cunning, strong, and relentless, he uses brute force, gadgets, and schemes to seize power, treasure, and the Kongs' bananas.

Lain Iwakura from Serial Experiments Lain – A quiet girl merging with the digital world. Gains near-omniscient knowledge, blurring the line between reality and cyberspace.

The Lemmings from the namesake game series – Small, green-haired creatures who mindlessly follow each other. Players guide them through hazards using clever tools to ensure they reach safety.

Madoka Kaname from Puella Magi Madoka Magica – A gentle, compassionate girl who ascends into a cosmic magical being. Wields reality-altering power fueled by hope and selflessness, reshaping the fate of magical girls across time and space.

Pegasus Seiya (Amaterasu Cloth) from Saint Seiya – A courageous Saint of Athena who wears the legendary Amaterasu Cloth. Fights with unwavering determination, channeling Cosmo energy to overcome overwhelming foes.

Psy-Crow from Earthworm Jim – A sadistic, scheming space bounty hunter. Uses cunning traps to pursue his enemies, especially Earthworm Jim.

Sabrewulf from Killer Instinct – A feral werewolf cursed with uncontrollable rage. Relies on speed, claws, and brute strength in battle.

Sailor Cosmos from Sailor Moon – A future incarnation of Sailor Moon. Guides timelines and wields immense cosmic power to preserve the universe.

Simon the Digger from Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann – A young digger turned universe-defying hero. Pilots mechas with unbreakable resolve and unstoppable spirit.

Tars Tarkas from the Barsoom series – A proud Green Martian warrior and chieftain. Fierce, honorable, and strategic, he wields great strength and combat skill while valuing loyalty, friendship, and a warrior’s code.

Tetsuo Shima from Akira – A psychic teenager whose powers spiral out of control. Gains near-limitless destructive abilities, but struggles with instability, ambition, and the loss of his humanity.
