My Favorite Characters by Franchise


Ben 10

Ben Tennyson - the main character, a boy who wields an alien watch-like device called the Omnitrix that enables him to transform into different alien beings, each with their unique set of powers and abilities

Heatblast - a Pyronite with fire and heat manipulation

Diamondhead - a Petrosapien with invulnerability and crystal manipulation

Grey Matter - a Galvan with super intelligence

XLR8 - a Kineceleran with super speed

Four Arms - a Tetramand with four arms and super strength

Upgrade - a Galvanic Mechamorph with shape-shifting, technology merging, enhancement, and manipulation

Ripjaws - a Piscciss Volann with underwater prowess, sharp teeth, and jaws that can tear through almost anything

Ghostfreak - an Ectonurite with flight, invisibility, intangibility, fear manipulation, and possession

Wildvine - a Florauna with elasticity and plant control

Way Big - a To'kustar with cosmic size, super strength, reflexes, and cosmic energy manipulation

Kevin 11 (from Ben 10: Alien Force and Ben 10: Ultimate Alien) - can absorb the properties of any material he touches, as well as absorb the biological powers of any superpowered being he touches

Humungosaur - a Vaxasaurian with super strength and the ability to grow larger

Jetray - an Aerophibian with flight, super speed, underwater breathing, and neuroshock beams

Big Chill - a Necrofriggian with flight, invisibility, intangibility, and cryokinesis via ice breath

Spidermonkey - an Arachnichimp with four arms, agility, surface-sticking, and web-spinning from its tail

Brainstorm - a Cerebrocrustacean with super intelligence and psionic lightning projection

Alien X - a Celestialsapien with nigh-omnipotence

Lodestar - a Biotsavartian with magnetokinesis and metal manipulation

Shocksquatch - a Gimlinopithecus with super strength, electrokinesis, electrical force fields, and telekinesis via electrical force fields

Feedback - a Conductoid with energy absorption, redirection, and manipulation

Gravattack - a Galilean with gravity manipulation, force fields, and telekinesis via force fields

Bloxx - a Segmentasapien with shape-shifting and body-altering

Azmuth - a Galvan from Galvan Prime and the creator of the Omnitrix. Renowned as one of the universe's most brilliant minds, he designed the Omnitrix to promote peace by allowing species to experience each other's lives

Bahrvad - a Tetramand gladiator from the planet Khoros. He possesses a red, muscular body with four arms and two pairs of eyes, characteristic of his species

Baz-L - a Galvanic Mechamorph, like Upgrade. He has a green and black striped body and can merge with technology to enhance its capabilities

Ben 10,000 - an alternate future version of Ben Tennyson who developed the Biomnitrix, a dual-Omnitrix system that allows him to fuse the DNA of two aliens into a single, hybrid form

Ben 23 - a version of Ben from Dimension 23, characterized by his use of the "Hero Watch" and a more self-serving approach to heroism

Blukic - a Galvan technician who, along with Driba, assists the Plumbers with technology and gadgets. He is slightly taller and thinner than the average Galvan

Driba - a Galvan technician working alongside Blukic. He is shorter and stockier, with a prominent chin and flat head, differing from most Galvans

Fourmungosaur - a fusion of Four Arms and Humungousaur's DNA, accessible only through the Biomnitrix. He has the body of Humungousaur with Four Arms' features, including four muscular arms and spikes. Only appears in the Ben 10: Omniverse episode "And Then There Were None"

Gar Red Wind - a Tetramand from the planet Khoros, similar to Four Arms. He possesses the species' characteristic four arms and red skin

Haplor - an Arachnichimp, Spidermonkey's species. He has the agility and web-slinging abilities typical of his kind

Helen Wheels - a human imbued with Kineceleran DNA, granting her super-speed abilities

Manny Armstrong - a human with Tetramand DNA, giving him enhanced strength and durability

Mechamorph Armor - a suit of armor made from Galvanic Mechamorph material, allowing the wearer to merge with and control technology

Mechamorph Guard - a robotic entity composed of Galvanic Mechamorph nanites, serving as a protector or enforcer

Mizaru - an Arachnichimp, like Spidermonkey. He exhibits the species' characteristic agility and web-slinging abilities

Princess Looma Red Wind - a Tetramand from the planet Khoros, similar to Four Arms. She possesses the species' characteristic four arms and red skin

Professor Paradox - a time-traveling scientist with control over space-time. Once trapped in the Void for millennia, he gained vast knowledge and temporal powers, often guiding Ben during major cosmic threats

Ship - a Galvanic Mechamorph, akin to Upgrade. He assists Julie Yamamoto and has the ability to merge with and enhance technology

Simian - an Arachnichimp, like Spidermonkey. He has the agility and web-slinging abilities typical of his kind

Starbeard - a Celestialsapien, like Alien X. He possesses immense reality-warping powers and a cosmic presence

Tetrax Shard - a Petrosapien, like Diamondhead. He is a bounty hunter known for his crystalline body and combat skills

The Galactic Gladiator - a Celestialsapien, akin to Alien X. He has vast cosmic powers and is known for his gladiatorial prowess

Tini - a Tetramand from the planet Khoros, like Four Arms

Yetta - a Gimlinopithecus, resembling Shocksquatch. She has the species' characteristic electrical abilities and appearance

Zs'Skayr/Ghostfreak - a Ectonurite, a species known for their ghostly appearance and abilities. Zs'Skayr is the evil counterpart of Ghostfreak

Malware - a Galvanic Mechamorph with malevolent intentions. He can merge with and corrupt technology, posing a significant threat

Khyber - a ruthless and skilled hunter who uses the Nemetrix, a device that turns him into predator versions of Ben’s aliens. He hunts powerful beings for sport and sees Ben as his ultimate prey

Bad Ben - an evil Ben Tennyson from an alternate timeline



The Elder Scrolls

Last Dragonborn/Dovahkiin (end of The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim) – A legendary hero born with the soul of a dragon and destined to slay Alduin. By the end of Skyrim, the Dragonborn becomes a godlike being capable of defeating world-ending threats, absorbing dragon souls, and mastering the Thu'um to manipulate reality.

Paarthurnax – An ancient dragon and former lieutenant of Alduin who turned against him. He teaches the Way of the Voice to mortals and seeks redemption through peace and wisdom.

The Greybeards (Arngeir, Borri, Einarth, and Wulfgar) – Monks who live in High Hrothgar and devote their lives to mastering and preserving the Way of the Voice. They guide the Dragonborn in their journey to understand the Thu’um.

Alduin – The World-Eater, a god-like dragon prophesied to destroy the world. He seeks to dominate all creation but is ultimately defeated by the Last Dragonborn.

Miraak (the first Dragonborn) – The first mortal to be gifted the Thu’um by the dragons. He betrayed them and sought power through Hermaeus Mora. He is encountered and defeated in the Dragonborn DLC.

Akatosh – The Dragon God of Time and chief deity of the Divines. Creator of the Dragonborn and father of time, he represents order and endurance in the cosmos.

The Altmer, or High Elves – A proud and magically gifted race from Summerset Isle. Known for their arrogance and skill in arcane arts, they often consider themselves superior to other races.

The Nerevarine - the protagonist of The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind. A prophesied reincarnation of the ancient hero Nerevar, destined to unite the tribes of Morrowind, defeat the godlike Dagoth Ur, and restore balance by mastering powerful magic, ancient secrets, and legendary combat skills.

Dagoth Ur - the antagonist of The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind. A godlike, immortal sorcerer corrupted by the Heart of Lorkhan, seeking to reshape Morrowind under his rule and spread his divine plague, ultimately defeated by the Nerevarine.

Anu – The primordial embodiment of stasis and order. Represents the essence of light and time, and is one of the original cosmic forces in Elder Scrolls mythology.

Anui-El – A reflection and extension of Anu. Serves as the soul of all things and the source of the Aedra, including Akatosh.

Divayth Fyr – An ancient and eccentric Telvanni wizard. He is over 4,000 years old and a powerful figure in Morrowind, known for studying Corprus disease.

Hermaeus Mora – The Daedric Prince of knowledge and memory. A tentacled being who hoards forbidden knowledge and seeks to control all information in existence.

Lord Harkon – The vampire lord antagonist of the Dawnguard DLC.

Lorkhan – The trickster god who persuaded the Aedra to create the mortal world. His actions led to his betrayal and the removal of his heart, which still influences events across Tamriel.

Magnus – The god of magic who designed Mundus, the mortal plane, but fled before its completion. His departure created the sun, and his essence powers the use of magic.

Namira – The Daedric Prince of darkness, decay, and all repulsive things. Revered by outcasts and those who embrace filth, rot, and the perverse.

Padomay – The primordial embodiment of chaos and change. Opposite to Anu, he represents instability and the potential for creation through conflict.

Satakal – A Yokudan deity who embodies the cyclical nature of destruction and rebirth. Viewed as the world and the devourer of the world.

Sotha Sil – One of the Tribunal gods. A brilliant inventor and mage, he created the Clockwork City and sought to understand and manipulate reality through logic and machinery.

Talos, or Tiber Septim – A mortal who ascended to godhood and became one of the Nine Divines. United Tamriel under the Empire and embodies heroism, conquest, and divine rule.

Vivec – A god-king of the Tribunal, known for his poetic and contradictory nature. He merged aspects of mortal and divine and ruled Morrowind with godly power and mysticism.



Dragon Ball

Goku - a pure-hearted Saiyan raised on Earth. Known for his love of fighting strong opponents, he’s mastered many forms, including Ultra Instinct

Vegeta - the proud Saiyan prince. Once a villain, now a hero fighting to surpass Goku. Known for his fiery temper and intense training

Gohan (adult) - The eldest son of Goku and Chi-Chi. Intelligent and powerful, especially in his Ultimate form, though he prefers peace over fighting.

Piccolo - a wise and powerful Namekian warrior. Former enemy turned mentor and protector, especially close to Gohan and Pan

Yamcha - a former desert bandit turned Z Fighter. Skilled in martial arts and ki techniques like the Wolf Fang Fist, but often outclassed by stronger foes

Bulma Briefs - a brilliant inventor and co-founder of Capsule Corp. Daughter of Dr. Briefs, the company’s founder. One of Goku’s oldest friends, Vegeta’s wife, and mother of Trunks. Key in creating useful tech

Kid Chi-Chi – the daughter of the Ox-King. Trained in martial arts, she’s shy and sweet but fiercely protective, and promises her childhood friend Goku that he’ll marry her.

Videl (Dragon Ball Z) - A strong-willed martial artist and daughter of Mr. Satan. She marries Gohan and becomes Pan’s mother.

Majin Buu (Good Buu/Fat Buu) – The kind-hearted, chubby version of Majin Buu. Loves sweets and becomes a friend and ally to the Z Fighters.

Pan - the granddaughter of Goku and daughter of Gohan and Videl. A young Saiyan-Human hybrid with great potential, energetic and determined.

Frieza - a ruthless galactic emperor known for destroying Planet Vegeta, the Saiyans' home planet. Has multiple powerful forms and a long-standing rivalry with Goku and Vegeta.

Nappa - a brutal Saiyan warrior and Vegeta’s former partner. Powerful during his debut but ultimately defeated by Goku.

Vegito – a fusion of Goku and Vegeta using the Potara earrings. Known for overwhelming strength and flashy techniques like the Final Kamehameha.

Broly (Dragon Ball Z) - a Legendary Super Saiyan with overwhelming power and a deep hatred for Goku. Known for his berserker rage and near-limitless strength.

Super Janemba - a powerful demon born from corrupted energy in the afterlife. Capable of warping reality, he is defeated by Gogeta, the fusion of Goku and Vegeta achieved by performing the Fusion Dance.

Bacterian - a disgusting martial artist who uses his foul stench as a weapon. Faced off against Krillin in the original World Martial Arts Tournament.

Bardock - Goku's father and a low-class Saiyan with a strong will. Gained visions of Frieza’s betrayal and tried to stop him before Planet Vegeta's destruction.

Gine - Goku’s gentle and caring mother. Unlike most Saiyans, she values family and emotion over battle.

Broly (Dragon Ball Super) - a reimagined version of Broly with a tragic backstory. His raw power surpasses most fighters, and he becomes an ally after battling Goku and Vegeta.

Cheelai - a kind-hearted former Frieza soldier who befriends Broly. Helps rescue him from Frieza's control and supports his new life.

Legendary Super Saiyan 2 Kefla - the fused warrior of Caulifla and Kale taken to the next level. Her power is immense, combining Kale’s rage and Caulifla’s aggression.

Ranfan – A female martial artist who competed in the World Martial Arts Tournament. Uses distraction tactics in battle but lacks serious combat power. Bold and confident.

Xeno Goku (base and Super Saiyan 4 Limit Breaker forms) - A time-patrol version of Goku from the Dragon Ball Heroes timeline. Uses Super Saiyan 4 Limit Breaker form, granting immense power in defending the timelines.

Xeno Vegeta (Super Saiyan 4 Limit Breaker) - A time-patrol version of Vegeta from the Dragon Ball Heroes timeline. Fights alongside Xeno Goku and reaches Limit Breaker form, rivaling gods in strength.

Xeno Broly - a version of Broly from the Dragon Ball Heroes timeline. More controlled and even more powerful, often used in multiversal threats.

Zalama - the divine being who created the Super Dragon Balls. Extremely mysterious and possibly one of the most powerful entities in the Dragon Ball multiverse.



Transformers

Optimus Prime (base and Matrix of Leadership versions)

Bumblebee – A quick and clever Autobot scout. Brave and friendly, always ready to help.

Arcee – A skilled female Autobot warrior. Agile and determined with strong loyalty.

Grimlock – The powerful Dinobot leader with a fierce T-Rex mode. Strong-willed and battle-hungry.

B'Boom - a Maximal whose beast mode is a mandrill

Blurr - A lightning-fast Autobot messenger. Sharp-tongued and quick-witted with unmatched speed.

Cosmos - an Autobot whose alternate mode is a UFO

Glyph - A small, intelligent Autobot known for her linguistic and archaeological expertise. Prefers research over combat but is capable when needed.

Highbrow - A scholarly Autobot who values logic and knowledge. Transforms into a twin-rotor helicopter and often analyzes problems from an intellectual angle.

Hot Rod – A brave Autobot with a fiery spirit. Quick, skilled, and destined for leadership.

Metroplex - A colossal Autobot city-bot. Serves as a mobile base and powerhouse in battle, often silent but fiercely protective of his allies.

Optimus Minor – A spirited Maximal scout and warrior. Transforms into a spider monkey.

Optimus Primal - leader of the Maximals. Transforms into a gorilla.

Perceptor - super-intelligent Autobot with a microscope alternate mode

Primus - The creator god of the Transformers, embodying order and light. Opposes Unicron and created Cybertron along with the original Transformers.

Prowl – A strategic Autobot tactician known for his calm, logical mind. Skilled in planning and analysis, he values order and efficiency, often acting as Optimus Prime’s second-in-command.

Rewind - A small Autobot archivist and data collector. Often partners with Eject and is known for his vast memory banks and analytical mind.

Seaspray - An Autobot who transforms into a hovercraft. Known for his love of the sea and his distinct gurgling voice due to his aquatic form.

Sky Lynx - A proud and boastful Autobot who transforms into a space shuttle and lynx-like beast. Highly capable and values his own eloquence and achievements.

Springer - an Autobot who is also a Wrecker with two alternate modes: a land vehicle and a helicopter vehicle with wheels.

Megatron (base and Dark Energon-powered versions)

Starscream (base and Underbase versions)

Soundwave – A loyal Decepticon spy and communications expert. Calm, calculating, with deadly precision.

Skywarp - A Decepticon Seeker who transforms into a jet. Known for his teleportation ability and cowardly, prank-loving nature. Loyal to Megatron but often unreliable.

Predaking - A powerful Predacon combiner with fierce beast modes. Known for savage strength and relentless hunting instincts.

Sixshot - A powerful Decepticon with six different transformations. Known as a one-robot army, he's cold, efficient, and nearly unstoppable in combat.

Tarantulas – A cunning Predacon scientist and schemer. Transforms into a venomous tarantula. Known for his intelligence, cruelty, and obsession with experiments.

Trypticon - A massive Decepticon city-bot. Transforms into a battle station or dinosaur-like form, often used for destruction and conquest.

Gas Skunk – A devious Predacon warrior. Transforms into a skunk. Known for his foul chemical attacks, stealthy sabotage, and unpredictable, nasty temperament.

Unicron - A malevolent god of chaos and destruction. Transforms into a planet-sized being and seeks to consume worlds, including Cybertron.

Prima - The first Transformer created by Primus. Wields the Star Saber and represents leadership, valor, and righteousness among the original Thirteen.

Vector Prime - One of the original Thirteen Transformers. Guardian of time and space, he ensures the multiverse remains balanced and stable.
